As of Tuesday, people in Ontario can buy beer at 58 locations across the province in a long-awaited move that was announced by Premier Kathleen Wynne at a Toronto Loblaws.

In the initial rollout, 25 grocery stores in the Greater Toronto Area will be licensed to sell beer, in addition to 16 in southwestern Ontario, 13 in the east and six in the north.

Two of the stores that will sell beer have yet to be listed by the province.

Below is a list of all 58 locations listed by the provincial government that will be selling beer.

The province is advising people who want to buy beer to first to check with the grocery stores before heading there.
